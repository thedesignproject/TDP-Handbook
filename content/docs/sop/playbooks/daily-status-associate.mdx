## Your Role as Associate

As an Associate, you're responsible for:
- Posting daily status updates for each customer
- Asking for help when you need it
- Booking Office Hours proactively
- Posting work questions in customer channels (not DMs)

This guide shows you exactly how to do each of these.

## 2. Start of Day Routine

### Check for Lead's Morning Context

**First thing each day:** Check the customer internal channel for any morning context from your Lead.

Your Lead will post if:
- Customer priorities shifted
- Direction changed overnight
- You need to redirect work before starting

**If there's no message:** Proceed with your planned work from yesterday's sync.

## 4. Asking for Feedback

### Best Practice: Ask Specific Questions

**Instead of:**
> "Let me know what you think" + Loom link

**Use:**
> "Should I go with the tab navigation or sidebar approach? Context in this Loom: [link]"

**Why:** Specific questions get faster, more useful responses.

## 5. Channel Usage Rules

**Golden Rule:** All work questions go in customer channels, not DMs.

### Where to Post

| Channel | When to Use |
|---------|-------------|
| **#internal-[customer]** | Daily sync, work questions, design feedback, Loom reviews, project questions |
| **Office Hours** | Real-time blockers, complex discussions, walking through work together |
| **DMs with Lead** | Personal/sensitive topics, performance conversations, "can we talk?" pings |

### Why Customer Channels?

- Creates searchable history
- Lead and customer can both see context
- Other team members can learn from discussions
- Nothing gets lost in DMs

**If your Lead responds to a work question in DMs:** They'll redirect you to post it in the customer channel instead. This is to help you build the right habit.

## 6. Office Hours

Your Lead holds 2-3 bookable 30-min slots each week. Use them proactively.

### When to Book Office Hours

**Book when:**
- Blocked and need real-time problem-solving
- Design decision requires discussion (not just async feedback)
- Need to walk through something complex together
- Customer situation needs alignment

**Don't book when:**
- Question can be answered in daily sync thread
- Just need approval (use async Loom + thread)
- Status update (use daily sync)

### Expected Booking Frequency

| Your Phase | Expected Usage |
|------------|----------------|
| **Months 1-2** | Minimum 2x/week |
| **Months 3-5** | 0-2x/week as needed |
| **Month 6+** | 0-1x/week as needed |

**If you're in months 1-2 and not booking Office Hours:** Your Lead will check in. Not booking is a red flag ‚Äî it means you're either not asking for help when you need it, or you're stuck and hiding it.

### How to Book

Use your Lead's **cal.designproject.io** link (they'll share it with you).

## 7. Edge Case: Slow Days or Nothing to Report

### Rule: Always Post. Never Skip the Daily Sync.

Even on slow days, the status update provides visibility. "Nothing to report" without context is not acceptable.

### What to Post on Slow Days

| Scenario | Example Status Update |
|----------|----------------------|
| **Blocked / waiting** | "What I did: Waiting on [X] to unblock the dashboard work. Reviewed competitor patterns while blocked. Status: üî¥ Blocked. Tomorrow: Resume dashboard once unblocked." |
| **Light workload** | "What I did: Wrapped up minor fixes from yesterday. No new tasks assigned. Status: ‚ö†Ô∏è Need input ‚Äî ready for next assignment. Tomorrow: [pending direction]" |
| **Sick / partial day** | "What I did: Out sick this morning, back half-day. Made progress on [X]. Status: ‚úÖ On track (adjusted). Tomorrow: Continue [X]." |
| **Slow day** | "What I did: Slower day ‚Äî worked on [X] but didn't finish. Status: ‚ö†Ô∏è Need input ‚Äî may need to adjust timeline. Tomorrow: Finish [X], start [Y]." |

### Why This Matters

- Your Lead can't help if they don't know there's a problem
- Patterns become visible early (before they're crises)
- You practice owning your situation, not hiding from it

**Never skip the daily sync.** Even if you have nothing to report, post that you have nothing to report and why.
