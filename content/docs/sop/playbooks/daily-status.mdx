## Purpose

This playbook defines how Leads and Associates communicate daily. The goal is simple: **clear visibility, fast feedback, no surprises.**

Every Associate knows what's expected. Every Lead knows how to respond. Everyone knows where communication happens.

## 3. Channel Usage Rules

All work discussions stay in customer channels. This creates searchable history and shared context.

### Where to Communicate

| Channel | Use For | NOT For |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **#internal-[customer]** | Daily sync, work feedback, design direction, Loom reviews, project questions, morning context | â€” |
| **Office Hours** | Blockers needing real-time conversation, complex design reviews, quick alignment syncs | Status updates, async questions |
| **DMs with Lead** | Personal/sensitive topics, performance conversations, "can we talk?" pings | Work questions, design feedback, project discussions |

### Redirecting Work Questions from DMs

If an Associate DMs a work question, Lead should **forward the message** to the customer channel and reply there:

1. Copy/forward the Associate's message to #internal-[customer]
2. Reply in the channel (not the DM)
3. Brief note to Associate: "Answered in the channel ðŸ‘†"

**Why this approach:** Less friction than asking them to re-post. You model the correct behavior while keeping the conversation documented. The Associate sees where the answer lives.

**Enforce consistently for 2-3 weeks** until the habit forms. Never answer work questions in DMs â€” it reinforces the wrong behavior and creates knowledge silos.

## 4. Office Hours

Office Hours replace ad-hoc 1-1s for tactical work discussions.

### Structure

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Owner** | Lead holds time, Associate books |
| **Frequency** | Weekly â€” Lead holds 2-3 bookable 30-min slots |
| **Booking** | Via Lead's Cal.com link |
| **Purpose** | Blockers, complex questions, design review discussions, quick alignment |

### Usage by Associate Phase

| Phase | Expected Usage | Lead Expectation |
|-------|----------------|------------------|
| **Months 1-2** | Minimum 2x/week | If Associate isn't booking, check in â€” no bookings is a red flag |
| **Months 3-5** | As needed | 0-2x/week is typical |
| **Month 6+** | As needed | Should decrease as autonomy increases, 0-1x/week typical |

### When to Book Office Hours

**Book when:**
- Blocked and need real-time problem-solving
- Design decision requires discussion (not just async feedback)
- Need to walk through something complex together
- Customer situation needs alignment

**Don't book when:**
- Question can be answered in daily sync thread
- Just need approval (use async Loom + thread)
- Status update (use daily sync)

```

     (buffer)            (buffer)
```

**Example timeline:**
- Associate deadline: Wednesday 5pm
- Lead review deadline: Thursday 12pm (Lead blocks time)
- Customer presentation: Friday 2pm

### Lead Weekly Task Hygiene

Check weekly:
- [ ] All active tasks have an assigned Associate
- [ ] All tasks have internal deadline (Associate completes)

- [ ] Blocked tasks are flagged and addressed

## 6. Edge Cases

### Edge Case 1: Associate Has Nothing to Report

**Rule: Always post. Never skip the daily sync.**

Even on slow days, the status update provides visibility. "Nothing to report" without context is not acceptable.

| Scenario | Example Status Update |
|----------|----------------------|
| **Blocked / waiting** | "What I did: Waiting on [X] to unblock the dashboard work. Reviewed competitor patterns while blocked. Status: ðŸ”´ Blocked. Tomorrow: Resume dashboard once unblocked." |
| **Light workload** | "What I did: Wrapped up minor fixes from yesterday. No new tasks assigned. Status: âš ï¸ Need input â€” ready for next assignment. Tomorrow: [pending direction]" |
| **Sick / partial day** | "What I did: Out sick this morning, back half-day. Made progress on [X]. Status: âœ… On track (adjusted). Tomorrow: Continue [X]." |
| **Slow day** | "What I did: Slower day â€” worked on [X] but didn't finish. Status: âš ï¸ Need input â€” may need to adjust timeline. Tomorrow: Finish [X], start [Y]." |

**Why this matters:**
- Lead can't help if they don't know there's a problem
- Patterns become visible early (before they're crises)
- Associates practice owning their situation, not hiding from it

---

### Edge Case 2: Repeated Low/No Output â€” Lead Escalation

When Associate posts low or no progress for multiple consecutive days:

| Days | Signal | Lead Action |
|------|--------|-------------|
| **1 day** | Normal variance | Acknowledge, no action needed |
| **2 days** | Worth noting | Check in via thread: "Seeing a couple slower days â€” anything I can help unblock?" |
| **3 days** | Pattern forming | Book Office Hours or sync: "Let's talk through what's going on and how I can help." |
| **5+ days** | Performance concern | Escalate to Design Manager. Document pattern. Begin formal conversation. |

#### Response Templates

**Day 2 â€” Gentle check-in (in-thread):**
> Noticing a couple lighter days â€” is something blocking you, or do you need more work assigned? Let me know how I can help.

**Day 3 â€” Direct conversation (Office Hours):**
> Hey, I want to check in. I've seen a few days without much progress posted. No judgment â€” I just want to understand what's happening so we can fix it together. Is it:
> - Waiting on something from me or the customer?
> - Unclear on priorities or next steps?
> - Something personal going on?
> - Workload issue (too much or too little)?
>
> Let's figure it out.

**Day 5+ â€” Escalation to Design Manager:**

Document and share:
- Dates and what was posted
- Any context Associate provided
- What you tried (check-ins, Office Hours)
- Your assessment of root cause

**The goal is support, not punishment.** Most repeated low output has a fixable cause â€” unclear tasks, personal issues, skill gaps, or workload mismatch. Surface it early, address it directly.

### Edge Case 3: Associate Stops Posting Daily Sync Entirely

| Missed Days | Lead Action |
|-------------|-------------|
| **1 day** | DM: "Hey, didn't see your EOD update â€” everything okay?" |
| **2 days** | DM + mention in channel: "Checking in â€” please post your status update today." |

**Missing daily syncs is not acceptable.** Even "nothing to report" requires a post. Repeated no-shows without explanation should be escalated quickly.
