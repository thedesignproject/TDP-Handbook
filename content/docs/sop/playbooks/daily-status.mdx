## Purpose

This playbook defines how Leads and Associates communicate daily. The goal is simple: **clear visibility, fast feedback, no surprises.**

Every Associate knows what's expected. Every Lead knows how to respond. Everyone knows where communication happens.

## Overview: How It Works

### 1. Timezone Alignment

Before starting work together, Lead and Associate document working hours in Asana Project Overview:
- Associate working hours
- Lead working hours
- Overlap window for real-time availability

### 2. Daily Cadence

**Morning (Optional):**
- Lead posts context if priorities changed overnight
- Associate checks channel before starting work

**End of Day (Required):**
- Associate posts status update with: what they did, current status (‚úÖ‚ö†Ô∏èüî¥), links, feedback needed, tomorrow's plan
- Lead responds within 2 hours or by next morning

### 3. Office Hours

- Lead holds 2-3 bookable 30-min slots per week
- Associate books via cal.designproject.io when blocked or needs real-time discussion
- Expected usage: 2x/week minimum for months 1-2, decreasing as autonomy increases

### 4. Channel Usage

**All work discussions happen in customer channels (#internal-[customer]):**
- Daily status updates
- Work questions
- Design feedback
- Loom reviews

**DMs are only for personal/sensitive topics.**

---

## What Makes This Work

### For Associates:
- Clear expectations on what to post and when
- Quick feedback loop prevents being stuck for days
- Visible patterns of progress build trust
- Office Hours provide safety net for blockers

### For Leads:
- Daily visibility without micromanaging
- Early warning system for problems
- Documented history for context
- Clear escalation paths when patterns emerge
