title: "Associate - New Customer Assignment Checklist"
description: Step-by-step actions for Associates from customer assignment through first week of execution
---

**Use this checklist when:** You are assigned as Associate to a new customer.

**Timeline:** 3-5 business days from assignment to executing first task (actual days depend on customer calendar availability)

**Related SOP:** [Step 1: Onboarding](/docs/sop/process/onboarding) | [Step 2: Partnership Setup](/docs/sop/process/partnership-setup) | [Step 3: Customer Kickoff](/docs/sop/process/customer-kickoff)

---

## Day 0: Assignment & Access Confirmation

<Callout type="info">
**Trigger:** Design Manager assigns you to a new customer and sets up your workspace access. Lead will reach out to schedule partnership setup.
</Callout>

### Your Actions

- [ ] **Receive assignment notification** from Design Manager
- [ ] **Confirm workspace access** (set up by Design Manager):
  - Asana project access
  - Figma project access
  - Customer Slack channel access
  - Fireflies/meeting recordings access
- [ ] **Review customer documentation** available in Knowledge Tab:
  - Sales context and business goals
  - Customer Success expectation meeting notes
  - Any existing design files or brand assets
  - Customer communication preferences
- [ ] **Prepare for partnership setup:**
  - Note any questions about customer context
  - Review your current workload and capacity
  - Understand this is a Foundation phase customer (Months 1-3)

**Reference:** [Associate Responsibilities](/docs/sop/roles/associate) (80% Design Execution, 10% Communication & Learning, 10% Team Collaboration)

---

## Day 1: Documentation Review & Question Preparation

<Callout type="info">
Timeline: 1-2 days for initial onboarding review before partnership setup session.
</Callout>

### Your Actions

- [ ] **Deep dive into customer documentation:**
  - Read all materials in Asana Knowledge Tab
  - Review any existing Figma files
  - Understand customer industry and product
  - Note customer stage and project complexity
- [ ] **Prepare context questions for Lead:**
  - What are the customer's top priorities?
  - What's the expected workload and timeline?
  - What's the customer's communication style?
  - What are the quality standards for this customer?
  - What should I focus on first?
- [ ] **Review role expectations:**
  - [What Associates are responsible for](/docs/sop/roles/associate)
  - [What can be delegated to you](/docs/sop/roles/lead#what-can-be-delegated-to-associates)
  - [What Lead retains ownership of](/docs/sop/roles/lead#what-leads-own-cannot-delegate)
- [ ] **Wait for Lead to schedule partnership setup session**

**Reference:** [Step 1: Onboarding](/docs/sop/process/onboarding) (Associate prepares context questions for the Lead)

---

## Day 2-3: Partnership Setup Session with Lead

<Callout type="warn">
This is a 30-minute session with your Lead BEFORE the customer kickoff meeting. Come prepared with questions.
</Callout>

### Session Focus (Based on Step 2: Partnership Setup)

- [ ] **Understand Cal.com setup:**
  - Learn how the customer books meetings
  - Understand your role in scheduling
- [ ] **Absorb customer context from Lead:**
  - Customer business goals and priorities
  - Project scope and current stage
  - Key stakeholders and decision-makers
  - Customer communication preferences
- [ ] **Clarify communication expectations:**
  - Daily EOD update requirements (Loom or Asana)
  - How to ask questions (Slack for quick, Asana for task-related)
  - Response time expectations
  - Weekly check-in cadence (if applicable)
- [ ] **Understand role boundaries for this customer:**
  - What you own: Execution, daily updates, iterations, dev handoff
  - What Lead owns: Strategy, customer meetings, final quality approval
  - Decision framework: "Strategy vs. Execution"
- [ ] **Learn success metrics:**
  - Quality standards for this customer
  - Your growth phase: Foundation (Months 1-3)
  - Revision expectations: Max 2 revisions per deliverable
  - Path to increased autonomy
- [ ] **Confirm feedback cadence:**
  - Daily feedback after EOD updates or customer meetings
  - Bi-monthly structured feedback every two weeks
- [ ] **Ask all prepared questions** from your documentation review

**Reference:** [Step 2: Partnership Setup](/docs/sop/process/partnership-setup)

---

## Before Kickoff: Preparation

<Callout type="info">
Your attendance at the kickoff meeting is OPTIONAL. Lead will decide if you should attend based on project needs.
</Callout>

### If You're Attending Kickoff

- [ ] **Understand your role in the meeting:**
  - Lead owns and facilitates the meeting
  - You observe and learn customer voice and dynamics
  - You may ask clarifying questions when appropriate
  - Focus: Understanding customer priorities and communication style
- [ ] **Review kickoff objectives** with Lead:
  - What are the meeting goals?
  - What should you listen for?
  - When is it appropriate to ask questions?
- [ ] **Prepare to take notes:**
  - Customer priorities
  - Communication preferences
  - Project scope clarifications
  - Any technical details relevant to your work

### If You're NOT Attending Kickoff

- [ ] **Confirm you'll receive the meeting recording:**
  - Lead will share Fireflies/Granola recording
  - Commit to watching within 24 hours
  - Prepare to submit questions after watching

**Reference:** [Meeting Attendance](/docs/sop/process/meeting-cadence#meeting-attendance) (Kickoff: Lead = Mandatory, Associate = Optional)

---

## When Scheduled: Kickoff Meeting & Post-Meeting Actions

### During Kickoff (If Attending)

- [ ] **Observe and learn:**
  - Customer communication style
  - Key priorities and goals
  - Stakeholder dynamics
  - Product vision and direction
- [ ] **Ask clarifying questions** when appropriate
- [ ] **Take detailed notes** on:
  - Customer preferences
  - Technical requirements
  - Design expectations
  - Timeline and milestones

### Post-Meeting (Whether You Attended or Not)

- [ ] **Watch full meeting recording** within 24 hours (NON-NEGOTIABLE)
- [ ] **Prepare post-meeting summary:**
  - Key takeaways about customer
  - Customer priorities identified
  - Questions for Lead about context or direction
  - Anything unclear from the discussion
- [ ] **Submit summary and questions** to Lead via Slack
- [ ] **Review Lead's answers** and clarifications
- [ ] **Update your understanding** of the customer and project

**Reference:** [Step 3: Customer Kickoff](/docs/sop/process/customer-kickoff) (Associate submits summary and questions to Lead) | [Associate Non-Negotiables](/docs/sop/roles/associate#non-negotiables) (Watch all customer meeting recordings within 24 hours)

---

## After Kickoff: Understand Meeting Cadence

<Callout type="info">
The ongoing communication structure is set up after kickoff based on customer preferences and schedule. Understand your attendance expectations.
</Callout>

### Your Actions

- [ ] **Learn the meeting structure** Lead has set up with this customer:
  - **Option 1:** Weekly scheduled meeting (1x 45 min OR 2x 30 min/week) — ideal for customers with predictable schedules
  - **Option 2:** Office hours (dedicated weekly time block) — for customers who travel frequently or have unpredictable schedules
  - Note: Customer's availability influences the cadence — be flexible
- [ ] **Understand your attendance requirements:**
  - **First few months:** You are REQUIRED to attend all customer meetings
  - **Lead attendance:** Recommended for first few months, optional after relationship established
  - Note: You may be leading these meetings solo as you progress
- [ ] **Add recurring meetings** to your calendar
- [ ] **Prepare for ongoing participation:**
  - Take notes during meetings
  - Ask questions when needed
  - Learn customer communication style
  - Build relationship with customer stakeholders

**Reference:** [Customer & Designer Meeting Cadence](/docs/sop/process/meeting-cadence) (Weekly/Office Hours: Associate = Required)

---

## Receive & Execute First Task

<Callout type="warn">
**Target:** Begin executing your first task within 3-5 business days of assignment, regardless of when kickoff happens. This is a Foundation phase deliverable.
</Callout>

### Your Actions

- [ ] **Receive first task assignment** from Lead in Asana:
  - Clear description and context
  - Reference materials and examples
  - Success criteria
  - Deadline
- [ ] **Confirm you understand the task:**
  - Review all provided context
  - Ask clarifying questions if anything is unclear
  - Confirm you have all resources needed
  - Verify deadline and expectations
- [ ] **Set up your work environment:**
  - Figma file structure
  - Asana task organization
  - Communication channels ready
- [ ] **Begin execution:**
  - Follow Lead's direction closely
  - Reference provided examples and templates
  - Focus on quality and attention to detail
  - Track your time and progress
- [ ] **Submit Daily EOD Update:**
  - What you worked on today
  - Progress made
  - Blockers or questions
  - Next steps planned
  - Format: Loom video or Asana comment

**Reference:** [Associate Responsibilities](/docs/sop/roles/associate) (80% Design Execution) | [Growth Framework](/docs/sop/growth-framework) (Month 1-3: Foundation — UI execution following exact direction)

---

## Ongoing: Iteration & Feedback Loop

### Daily Work Routine

- [ ] **Execute design work** (80% of your time):
  - Create high-quality designs based on Lead's direction
  - Follow provided templates and examples
  - Maintain design system standards
  - Focus on pixel-perfect execution
- [ ] **Submit EOD update every day:**
  - Loom video walkthrough of work OR Asana comment
  - Show progress, explain decisions, surface blockers
  - Submit by end of your workday
- [ ] **Receive and implement feedback from Lead:**
  - Daily feedback after EOD updates or customer meetings
  - Review feedback carefully
  - Ask clarifying questions if needed
  - Implement changes fully
  - Target: Max 2 revisions per deliverable (Foundation phase)
- [ ] **Watch all customer meeting recordings** within 24 hours:
  - Even if you attended live
  - Take detailed notes
  - Submit questions to Lead via Slack
- [ ] **Maintain communication standards:**
  - Proactive updates — no silent struggles
  - Surface blockers early
  - Ask questions when stuck
  - Document decisions in Asana/Figma

**Reference:** [Communication & Learning](/docs/sop/roles/associate#communication--learning-10-of-time) | [Non-Negotiables](/docs/sop/roles/associate#non-negotiables)

## Week 1 Checkpoint: Self-Assessment

### After First Deliverable Submission

- [ ] **Review your first week performance:**
  - Did I meet the quality standards?
  - Did I submit EOD updates daily?
  - Did I watch all meeting recordings within 24 hours?
  - Did I ask questions when stuck?
  - Did I meet the deadline?
- [ ] **Assess what you've learned:**
  - Do I understand the customer's priorities?
  - Do I know how to ask the Lead for guidance?
  - Am I comfortable with the tools and templates?
  - Do I understand the quality expectations?
- [ ] **Identify growth areas:**
  - What took longer than expected?
  - What do I need to improve?
  - What skills should I focus on developing?
- [ ] **Prepare for bi-monthly feedback** (coming in Week 2):
  - Reflect on your progress
  - Note questions about your growth path
  - Be ready to discuss what's working and what's not

**Reference:** [Growth Framework](/docs/sop/growth-framework) (Month 1-3: Foundation — Designs meet Lead approval within max 2 revisions)
