title: Expectations Meeting
description: Initial customer meeting to understand context, objectives, and set partnership foundations
---

**Owner:** Dianne (Customer Success Lead)
**Timing:** After customer signs up and payment is confirmed

## Purpose

The Expectations Meeting is conducted to understand the company context, business objectives, how design impacts those objectives, and to set the foundations for a good partnership. The information gathered is documented and handed off to the design team to set them up for success.

## Meeting Script

### Introduction

> "Intro Customer Success Lead.
>
> I'll be here from a Customer Success standpoint to make sure everything is running smoothly, and that we scale with your needs.
>
> I also step in as Fractional CDO with some of our customers that require it. That way, founders can focus on growth and operations while I make sure design decisions are strategic and cohesive.
>
> I have a couple questions before we get into processes, to understand your objectives and company a bit better."

---

## 1. Business Context

Understand the customer's current state and business trajectory:

**Walk me through your current product and user flow - what exists today?**

**What specific business metrics are you trying to move in the next 6 months, and how does design impact those?**

**Where are you in your business journey? Have you raised recently? Or are you planning to raise?**

---

## 2. Product Context

Identify product gaps and priorities:

**What's the biggest gap between your current product experience and where it needs to be in 12 months?**

<Callout type="info">
Based on Dianne's sales meeting transcript, ask 1-2 more specific questions about what they are looking for.
</Callout>

---

## 3. Team

Understand team structure and decision-making processes:

**How is your team structured? Who makes final design decisions, and what's the approval process today?**

**How is your team currently thinking about AI workflows? Are they integrating it to push designs?** (both design team if they have it or eng. team)

---

## 4. Success & Scoping

Set realistic expectations and understand past experiences:

**Have you worked with other design partners in the past? What are the things you didn't like about their process?**

**Any deadlines or objectives we need to take into account before kicking off?**

---

## 5. Team Introduction

Explain how TDP's team structure works:

**Your design team:** Super senior lead designer taking care of strategy + an associate designer taking care of the day to day execution. This way you get the best of both: quality and speed.

**Communication:**
- We work on Slack - ongoing communication
- 1 weekly meeting (45min) + a lot of async looms and showing progress
- 1 monthly touchbase with me to chat about any concerns, highlevel ideas, roadmap review or anything you need my help with

---

## 6. Housekeeping

Gather practical resources and access:

### Figma Space
- Will we be using yours or ours?
- Note: Seats are paid if it's in ours

### Resources to Request
- Any brand assets you can share?
- Documentation we need to review prior to kickoff?
- Company OKRs / KPIs?
- Roadmap?
- Figma screens?

---

## Post-Meeting

After the Expectations Meeting:

1. **Gather all source materials:**
   - Expectations Meeting notes and transcript
   - Sales meeting notes and transcript (from Dianne)
   - Customer website and public materials
   - External research (Crunchbase, etc.)

2. **Create the Asana Knowledge Hub:**
   - Follow the [Asana Knowledge Hub](/docs/customer-success/asana-knowledge-hub) process
   - Use the 10-section template to structure all customer context
   - Consolidate information from all sources into one comprehensive document

3. **Share with the design team** before their onboarding (Step 1 in the [Process Workflow](/docs/sop/process))

4. **Set up monthly check-ins** with the customer for ongoing CS support

<Callout type="info">
The [Asana Knowledge Hub](/docs/customer-success/asana-knowledge-hub) becomes the single source of truth for customer context, ensuring the design team has complete information before beginning their internal onboarding and customer kickoff process.
</Callout>
